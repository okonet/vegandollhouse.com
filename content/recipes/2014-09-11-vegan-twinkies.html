Vegan Twinkies

♫ Listening to: We by Felix Da Housecat ♫

Disclaimer: I normally wouldn't have posted these since they didn't come out perfect, but I'm participating in VeganMofo this month, and I *have* to post something every day. So, even thought the cream filling came out perfectly, I think the twinkie recipe could use improving.

 

Vegan Twinkie ingredients:

  • 1¼ cups organic unbleached flour
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 ½ tablespoons organic cornstarch
  • ½ tablespoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup soymilk (or substitute other nondairy milk)
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar (or substitute white vinegar)
  • ⅓ cup safflower oil
  • ¾ cups sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 

Vegan Twinkie directions:

Preheat the oven to 350°.

Coat the inside cubbies of a canoe pan using a paper towel with safflower oil on it.

Combine vinegar and soymilk, stir, and let sit.

Sift the flour, cornstarch, baking soda, baking powder, and salt in another bowl and whisk together.

Add oil, sugar, and vanilla to the soymilk mixture and stir well. Add dry ingredients into the wet and mix until smooth.

Fill the canoe cubbies about half full. I used ¼ cup of batter in each, and I think it was too much. It made my twinkies look like they were wearing a dorky hat. So, don't overfill yours accidentally! This is what too full looks like.

Bake for 19 minutes in the center rack (or until a toothpick inserted into the middle of one comes out clean).

This is what overfilled twinkies look like when they come out of the oven—stupid hats on all of them. Grrrrr.

 

Vegan Twinkie Cream Filling ingredients:

  • 1 can coconut cream (I buy mine from Trader Joe's, but unfortunately, it's not organic), left in the fridge overnight
  • ½ cup organic powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ teaspoon xantham gum

 

Vegan Twinkie Cream Filling directions:

Whisk the ingredients until smooth in the kitchenaid. You want it to form peaks that do not fall.

Transfer into a piping bag fitted with the Ateco Decorating Tip #231. (You can substitute any tip if you don't have this one, but keep in mind that the bigger the tip, the bigger hole it will leave at the bottom of your twinkie.)

Flip the twinkies onto their backs and poke them in their tummies about halfway through.

Do this in 3 or 4 places so you make sure the twinkies don't have any part without cream filling.
